\section{The main proof}

\begin{definition}
Let $T$ be a subset of $\R^n$. We say \emph{$g$ is a coordinate change on $T$} if $g \in C^1(T \rightarrow \R^n)$, $g$ is injective, and $J_g(x)$ is never zero.
\end{definition}

Here is our main result, which we intend to prove.

\begin{conj}
Let $X$ be a measurable subset of $T$, which is a subset of $R^n$. Suppose $g$ is a coordinate change on $T$, and let $f \in L(g(X))$. Then, ${f \circ g \cdot J_g \in L(X)}$ and

\[ \int_{g(X)} f =  \int_X f \circ g \cdot J_g \]
\end{conj}
