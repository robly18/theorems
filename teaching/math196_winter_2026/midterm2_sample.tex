\question There is exactly one choice of numbers $a$, $b$, and $c$ which make the following linear system of equations have exactly one solution. Determine these numbers, and find the solution in this scenario. Do not row-reduce the system.
\[
\left\{
\begin{array}{rrrrrrrr}
-6 x & + & 7 y & + & 4 z & = & 5 \\
-34 x & + & 39 y & + & 23 z & = & 28 \\
15 x & - & 17 y & - & 10 z & = & -12 \\
46 x & - & 53 y & - & 31z & = & a \\
11 x & - & 14 y & - & 8z & = & b \\
-18 x & + & 20 y & + & 12z & = & c \\
\end{array}
\right.
\]
The following matrix identities may be useful. You don't need to use both of them, they are both here to accommodate different ways of solving this problem.
\[
\begin{array}{ccc}
\begin{bmatrix}
-6 & 7 & 4 \\
-34 & 39 & 23 \\
15 & -17 & -10
\end{bmatrix}^{-1}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 5 \\
5 & 0 & 2 \\
-7 & 3 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
&
\begin{bmatrix}
 -6 & 7 & 4 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
 -34 & 39 & 23 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
 15 & -17 & -10 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
 46 & -53 & -31 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
 11 & -14 & -8 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\
 -18 & 20 & 12 & 0 & 0 & 1 \\
\end{bmatrix}^{-1}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
 1 & 2 & 5 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
 5 & 0 & 2 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
 -7 & 3 & 4 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
 2 & 1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
 3 & 2 & 5 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\
 2 & 0 & 2 & 0 & 0 & 1 \\
\end{bmatrix}
\end{array}
\]
